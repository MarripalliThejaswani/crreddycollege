Admission Overview

The Andhra Pradesh State Council of Higher Education (APSCHE) conducts and manages admissions to Pharmacy courses in the state through the AP EAPCET exam. This ensures a transparent, merit-based process for B.Pharmacy, M.Pharmacy, and Pharm.D admissions in government and private colleges across Andhra Pradesh.

At Sir C R Reddy College of Pharmaceutical Sciences, we offer comprehensive pharmacy programs with state-of-the-art facilities and experienced faculty to prepare students for successful careers in the pharmaceutical industry.

Programs Offered

B. Pharm

4 Years

Eligibility:

  • Indian citizens or PIO/OCI status holders
  • 10+2 with Biology, Physics, and Chemistry or Diploma in Pharmacy
  • Minimum age: 16 years (no upper age limit)
  • Minimum marks: 45% for general, 40% for SC/ST

Admission Process

1

Fee Payment

Pay the application fee online through Net Banking, Credit Card, or Debit Card. The fee is Rs 600 for OC, Rs 550 for BC, and Rs 500 for SC/ST candidates per stream. Save the Payment ID and Transaction ID after successful payment.

2

Fill Application

After successful fee payment, fill the application form using your Payment ID, Hall Ticket number, and other required details. All mandatory fields marked with asterisk (*) must be completed.

3

Certificate Verification

Submit required documents for verification either online or at designated Help Line Centers (HLCs). This includes your AP EAPCET Hall Ticket, Rank Card, marks memos, Transfer Certificate, study certificates, and category-specific documents.

4

Web Options Entry

Log in to the counseling portal to select your preferred colleges and courses in order of priority. Research and prioritize choices based on factors like location, faculty, infrastructure, and previous cutoffs.

5

Seat Allotment

Seats are allotted based on your AP EAPCET rank, reservation category, local/non-local status, gender, and special categories. Download the Provisional Allotment Letter from the official counseling website.

6

Reporting to College

Report to the allotted college within the stipulated time with the Provisional Allotment Letter and original certificates. Pay the prescribed tuition fee to confirm your admission.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the eligibility criteria for B.Pharm admission?

Candidates must be Indian citizens or hold PIO/OCI status and fulfill local/non-local status requirements. They must have completed or appeared for the final year of Intermediate (10+2) with Biology, Physics, and Chemistry as optional subjects, or have passed a Diploma in Pharmacy from a recognized institution. The minimum age required is 16 years as of December 31 of the admission year, with no upper age limit. General category candidates must secure at least 45% marks, while SC/ST candidates need a minimum of 40% marks.

How can I apply for M.Pharm program?

Candidates must have completed a B.Pharm degree from a recognized university with a minimum aggregate score of 55%. Admission is primarily through entrance examinations such as GPAT or AP PGECET. Applicants need to secure valid scores in these exams and participate in the centralized counseling process conducted by APSCHE, where seat allotment is based on entrance exam ranks and preferences.

What is the difference between Pharm.D and B.Pharm?

B.Pharm is a 4-year undergraduate program focused on pharmaceutical sciences, while Pharm.D is a 6-year doctorate program that includes extensive clinical training and patient care. Pharm.D graduates can practice as clinical pharmacists and provide direct patient care, similar to medical doctors. There's also a 3-year Post Baccalaureate Pharm.D program for those who already have a B.Pharm degree.

Is there any reservation policy for admissions?

Yes, admissions follow the reservation policy of the Government of Andhra Pradesh. Seats are reserved for candidates belonging to SC, ST, BC, and other categories as per the state regulations. Additionally, there are reservations for special categories like NCC, Sports quota, and Persons with Disabilities (PH).

What is the process for fee reimbursement?

Eligible students belonging to SC, ST, BC, EBC, and minority categories can apply for fee reimbursement as per the government schemes. Candidates need to submit an Income Certificate along with other required documents during certificate verification. The college will facilitate the reimbursement process as per the government guidelines.
